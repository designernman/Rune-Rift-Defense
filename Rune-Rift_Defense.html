About Rune Rift Defense

Rune Rift Defense is a solo‑developed tower defense game where you defend unstable rift paths using towers, global upgrades, and rune‑powered enemies.

The game is built to feel like a classic lane‑based tower defense, but with a focus on long‑term progression through upgrade currency and global buffs.

This project is created by an individual developer. If you have feedback, ideas, or bug reports, you can reach out at: atbankson@gmail.com.

Version Info

  • Current Version: v0.2 – Rune Enemies & Map Polish
  • Core Features: 3 maps, rune enemy types, global upgrades, in‑match upgrades, boss waves, Wooden Money Hut, stats, and weekly update plan.
  • Focus: Iterating on balance, readability, and progression pacing.

How to Play

Quick overview of how Rune Rift Defense works, including enemies, towers, currencies, and controls.

Basic Flow

  • From the Home screen, click Play, choose a map, then press Start.
  • In the game, open the Towers panel on the right, select a tower, then click on the map to place it.
  • Press Start Wave to begin a wave. Turn on Auto to automatically start new waves.
  • Use Pause and Speed to control the pace of the game.
  • Use the Fullscreen button in the top‑left of the map for an immersive view.

Currencies

  • 💵 In‑Map Currency (Money): Earned by defeating enemies during a run. Used to place towers, upgrade towers (up to 8 times each), and buy/upgrade the Wooden Money Hut.
  • ⭐ Upgrade Currency (Meta Currency): Earned by surviving 50 waves (currently $150 per 50 waves). Used outside the match to buy permanent global upgrades that boost tower stats across all runs.

Enemy Types (Rune Shapes)

  • Circle – Basic Rune: Normal HP, normal speed. Soft blue glow.
  • Triangle – Swift Rune: Low HP, high speed. Teal glow with sharper edges.
  • Square – Bulwark Rune: High HP, slow speed. Heavy orange glow.
  • Diamond – Rift Boss: Very high HP, slow but dangerous. Pulsing purple rune aura.

Tower Shapes

  • Dart: Circle tower.
  • Cannon: Square tower.
  • Sniper: Triangle tower.
  • Flamethrower: Rectangle tower.
  • Railgun: Diamond tower.
  • Wooden Money Hut: Hut‑shaped income tower.

Global Upgrades

  • Global upgrades cost 2.5× more than the base design.
  • Each global upgrade level gives a 2× stat boost (damage, range, fire rate, etc.).
  • Global upgrades apply to all runs and stack multiplicatively.
  • Access them from the Upgrades tab on the top bar.

In‑Match Tower Upgrades

  • Click a placed tower to open its upgrade panel at the bottom of the map.
  • Each tower can be upgraded up to 8 times during a run.
  • Each upgrade increases stats and costs more than the last.
  • These upgrades reset every game and stack on top of global upgrades.

Wooden Money Hut (Bank Tower)

  • Costs $300 to place.
  • Generates $100 at the end of every wave per level.
  • Can be upgraded only inside the match (up to level 5), increasing income by +100 per level.
  • Must be repurchased every game and is not affected by global upgrades.

Placement & Ghost Colors

  • When placing a tower, a ghost appears where your cursor is.
  • Green ghost: Valid placement and you have enough money.
  • Red ghost: Invalid placement or not enough money.
  • You can’t place towers too close to the path, on obstacles, or on top of other towers.

Collapsible Tower Panel

  • The tower panel is on the right side of the game screen.
  • Use the Towers ⮝ / Towers ⮟ button to open or collapse it.
  • When collapsed, you get more map space; when open, you can pick towers easily.
